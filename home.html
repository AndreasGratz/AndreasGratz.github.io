Follow my Story
(Hover with the mouse over a picture and the text will appear)

High School of a biotechnological orientation (till 2009)

From childhood onwards I have always been interested in science, awestruck by nature's complexity with layer beneath layer until one arrives at the atom, whereas I see similarities in IT with bits as the core components behind the frontend processes visible to a user. This observation is even more intriguing given the increasing coalescence of both disciplines, biotech and IT, as documented by the famous writer Yuval Noah Harari.

One-year Voluntary Community Service in Norway (till 2010)

After having achieved the General Qualification for University Entrance (Abitur), I wanted to get to know another country with a different language and culture. I decided for the European Voluntary Service, funded by the EU, and went to Norway. There, I worked both for a countryside education center und a well-known cancer rehabilitation facility. This picture is taken before the arrival of our patients for a weekly outing in the nearby mountains.

Bachelor studies in Konstanz (from 2011 till 2015)

Although I had a life science background from my studies at High School, I decided to pursue my Bachelor studies in Politics and Public Administration at the University of Konstanz, part of the small group of aspiring universities in the Excellence Initiative of the German government. Already during my High School Studies I had been the outstanding student in Social Sciences and wanted to deepen my knowledge at this breathtakingly beautiful location.

Internship at the German Center CPG in Bangkok

After I had my Erasmus exchange studies at Université de la Nouvelle-Calédonie on the French island of New Caledonia in the Pacific Ocean, I moved to Bangkok for my internship in the fall of 2013. There, I worked for five months with the CPG which is a pioneering cross-national association of three faculties of law of German universities, and the faculty of law of Thammasat University. I helped plan and manage conferences as well as publications of the center.

Caseworker for Refugees with the JRS in Bangkok (till November 2015)

Both my studies in Social Science and my internship in Bangkok had a decidedly positive influence on me and so I decided to combine the two, working as a social worker with refugees in Bangkok right after the completion of my Bachelor studies. The work was difficult and my clients often faced very trying circumstances. The picture is taken from an English class for refugees from Somalia which I initiated and managed.

Social Counselor for Refugees with Caritas in Germany (till June 2017)

After having worked with refugees in Thailand, I returned to Karlsruhe in Germany amid the 2015/2016 influx of people in need from various national backgrounds. I counseled and helped asylum seekers and refugees with various issues and expended considerable effort for the initiation and management of a local alliance of organizations. The picture is taken from an event in Karlsruhe at which our alliance was present.

CEMS Master Studies in Helsinki (from 2017 till 2020)

Working as a social worker, I had found that I flourish when organizing and managing projects and when facilitating team efforts. Therefore, I decided to pursue my Master studies in Management, enrolling for a double degree with the prestigious CEMS degree. As part of my studies, I expanded my skills in statistics and coding acquired through my Bachelor studies and generally focused on the topic of Digitalization, having several relevant jobs beside my studies. I am currently finalizing my Thesis.

CEMS Exchange Studies in Santiago de Chile (Spring of 2019)

While still working remotely for two jobs in Finland, I went to Santiago de Chile for my exchange studies. I expected that Chile would be an adventure and I found the countryside to be breathtaking. I also enjoyed the company of other CEMS students from around the world. Importantly, with a group of students I worked on a three-month long project for a big MNC in the shipping industry to which I contributed my statistical and analytical expertise.

Today, a consultant in Germany, supporting companies in their efforts at digitalization

In June I started working for Ayudantes GmbH in Hamburg, Germany, which is a young and aspiring consulting firm focusing on Digitalization and all the challenges that arise from it. Until recently, I have managed and implemented a project that centered around streamlining management processes using Microsoft's Power Platform.
